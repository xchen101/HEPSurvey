Your attitude toward Open Science

Open Science aims to increase the transparency of research process and reusability of research output. 
For the purpose of the survey, please consider the following definitions of some of the main concepts in Open Science, these terms will be appearing repeatedly throughout the survey. 
Open Access (OA): Publishing and dissemination models that ensure the free and open online access to full-text peer-reviewed scientific papers for everyone. 
Data / code sharing: The act of occasional sharing of a code snippet or data sample with a limited group or individual that do not have access to them by default (e.g. researchers who are not your immediate collaborators).
Open data: Data that is made freely and openly available and accessible for reuse, alongside or after the publication of research paper.
Open software: Research software that is developed and managed in open repositories under open licenses, so that anyone can freely access and reuse the software.
